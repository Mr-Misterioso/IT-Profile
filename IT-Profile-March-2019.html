One Paragraph Questions

1. What is your interest in IT? When did it start? What started your interest? What are your experiences?

In early high school I chose ICT as electives and I was introduced to different areas of IT. First was robotics and how to use modules to program, next was A.I. and Scratch which both use a similar programming style. Then I learnt how to use HTML and CSS. I have been intrigued by the different programming styles and their applications in the world ever since.

2. What makes IT interesting for you?

IT has a vast array of purposes. It can be useful for making long distance surgery by operating surgical robots or, to apply for a job. Along with its functions, there are many different forms of code and they are quite versatile. For example, python can be used to make complex systems or mechanisms, and also in factory processes or business applications.

3. Why did you begin to study at R.M.I.T?

I began to study at R.M.I.T in order to broaden my knowledge on IT; I want a better understanding it and to become inspired to work towards a career in a field of IT.

4. What do you expect to learn from this course?

From this course, I expect to learn more about the IT industry, where I can fit in, and types of work that I might be interested in doing.

5. What are your plans after the course?

My plans after this course are to go to University full-time and learn more about a specific IT field that interests me. My end goal would be to find a job that utilises the IT skills that I have learned.

My Ideal Job

Software Engineer

A software engineer is someone who creates designs, develops and improves computer programs that we use every day. This includes applications for businesses, social media, and operating and network systems.

As a Software Engineer at ‘Lockheed Martin Australia’, I would be providing guidance about software engineering to different stakeholders in the company, be involved in investigations on systematic issues, and designing or re-designing existing systems.

It would be a job where I would be assisting company workers in doing their job and making systems or existing systems more effective and comfortable to use. I would like to have a job like this because I enjoy finding solutions to problems and building my own programs/systems to help others.

The knowledge/experience required for a job like this are:

  • Python/C and JavaScript programming
  • Application development skills in a Linux environment
  • User interface development
  • Experience in Radar Signal Processing
  • Rapid prototyping skills

I have experience in programming, however, it’s not in these areas. I have done HTML, CSS, and modular programming, but I don’t know how to utilise Python. From my current knowledge I would be able understand how to write with it, but I would need to learn it properly. To learn these skills, I would take further training.
